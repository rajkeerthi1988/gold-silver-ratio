Historical Trend

The Gold-Silver Ratio has historically ranged between 40 and 100. Extreme highs often signal silver buying opportunities, while extreme lows favor gold accumulation.

What is the Gold-Silver Ratio?

The Gold-Silver Ratio measures how many ounces of silver are needed to buy one ounce of gold. Traders use it to identify relative value opportunities between the two metals.

FAQ

What is a good gold-silver ratio?
Typically between 60–70 is considered balanced.

When should I buy silver?
When the ratio is above 80, silver may be undervalued.

When should I buy gold?
When the ratio is below 60, gold may be undervalued.
