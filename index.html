What Is the Gold Silver Ratio?

The Gold-Silver Ratio measures how many ounces of silver are required to buy one ounce of gold. It is calculated using the formula:

Gold Price ÷ Silver Price

Investors monitor this ratio to identify potential opportunities between gold and silver. A high ratio may indicate silver is undervalued, while a low ratio may indicate gold is undervalued.

Typical Gold Silver Ratio Zones

  • Above 80 → Silver may be undervalued
  • 60 – 80 → Neutral range
  • Below 60 → Gold may be undervalued

Why Investors Watch the Ratio

Precious metals investors use the gold silver ratio to help decide when to switch between gold and silver holdings. Historically, when the ratio becomes extremely high, silver has often outperformed gold afterward.
